Overview

Assessment type: DX (documentation experience), v3 «Architect's Bar (compact)» format — v2 trimmed to what survived one full cycle. Formation date: 2026-06-12. Snapshot taken against main at commit 2a528d7 (Merge #55 fix/bugs-v2). Previous baseline: 2026-06-02.
Headline: discipline kept its altitude (A−/A across IA, ADRs, tooling, UI Kit); the only material score-mover was BL-064 (search migration to Pagefind, ADR 0033) which closed the two-cycle stall on row 14. Five ADRs ratified since the last cycle (0030 token contract, 0031 foundations canon, 0032 catalog-first IA, 0033 Pagefind, 0034 staging stand) — none of them are substance: they are governance scaffolding. The operational-liveness cap is intact: postmortem corpus has not grown in five weeks, no SLO burn-history dashboard, time-to-first-PR uninstrumented for a third consecutive cycle, feedback widget still writes only to localStorage.

Scope and methodology

Scope

In scope: the internal Diátaxis quadrants (Tutorials, How-to, Reference, Explanation), the onboarding/ hub, navigation tree, search (now Pagefind per ADR 0033), design tokens / UI Kit v2 (assets_v2/, contract pinned by ADR 0030), authoring templates (handbook/templates/), CI gates (make ci, make docs-check, per-service Makefiles after ADR 0028 Phase 1, tools/docs/validate_docs_*.py, .github/workflows/a11y-formal-checks.yml), the auto-mounted feedback & edit-on-github widgets, the governance backlog, the substance corpus from Vol. 2 (architecture narrative, three postmortems, threat model, capacity snapshot, data invariants, 35 ADRs, 4 RFCs), and the post-Vol.2 IA refactor-drift remediation waves. Out of scope: public marketing site and product UI.

Methodology

Two tracks, cross-linked inside one HTML file: (A) the same 25-row rubric Table 1 / Table 2 as 2026-06-02 with the Δ column anchored to the 2026-06-02 baseline; (B) ten architect-bar axes graded A+ … F with a one-line justification each plus a single verdict paragraph. The Δ column in Top-5 gaps cross-links to the Bar axis that explains the gap. Substance vs Form is the dominant filter: discipline (Diátaxis quadrants, ADR coverage by count, tooling pipelines, UI Kit) is form; rejected-alternatives with rooted reasons, postmortem corpus from real incidents, capacity numbers, threat model with adversary tiers, «would we do this again?» retrospectives are substance. Discipline-A− / substance-C overlays do not round up to A−. Every score names a file, ADR, RFC, script, or workflow; where the claim rests only on visual inspection it is marked not-confirmed.

Summary verdict

~B+ on the architect's scale (unchanged from 2026-06-02). Discipline went up another half-step inside the B+ ceiling — Pagefind closed the two-cycle search stall (Axis 5 / Axis 8), service-folder split + per-service Makefiles took Axis 8 from A− into A, ADR 0032 catalog-first IA stress-tested IA scalability into A−. Substance moved backwards on the single axis that matters most: Axis 4 (operational maturity) slipped from B to B− because the postmortem corpus has not grown in five weeks and there is still no live SLO burn-history dashboard — retro discipline without a live signal decays by default. Five new ADRs (0030/0031/0032/0033/0034) are all governance scaffolding, not substance: they pin contracts, name conventions, codify pre-existing practice. The remaining gap to A− is not more discipline — it is operational liveness (a feedback loop that closes end-to-end, a postmortem from a real incident in the last cycle, a time-to-first-PR number that exists), threat model adversary tiers, and a first-person «would we do this again?» retrospective on at least three of the 35 ADRs.
