Foundations handbook canon — Explanation under reference/, lens-chip vocabulary

Pages under foundations/reference/<discipline>/ (SA, SWE, QA, Dev) that explain methodology and principles are Diátaxis Explanation pages, not Reference. They declare data-page-type="explanation", carry a single lens chip on every numbered section (one of seven: stance · rules · rationale · anti · apply · exemplars · explain) and close with a «What to read next» pointer. Reference data — live matrices, audit results, decisions — lives in governance/, not in the handbooks.

Context

The internal portal hosts methodology handbooks under foundations/reference/<discipline>/. The SA handbook (handbook/) has ~24 pages today; SWE and QA handbooks (foundations/reference/swe/, foundations/handbook/qa/) are stubbed and will grow on the same pattern.

Two implicit conventions have emerged on these pages over the last two months but were never written down:

  1. Diátaxis quadrant mismatch with folder name. Pages live under a folder literally named reference/, but their content is Explanation — rationale, trade-offs, mental models, worked examples. They've been declaring data-page-type="explanation" in the body tag, contradicting the folder. A new author looking at the folder name first will revert to "reference" because the folder told them so.
  2. Section lens chips. A vocabulary of seven small chips (data-lens="stance|rules|rationale|anti|apply|exemplars|explain") has spread across these pages to mark each section by its rhetorical mode — «this section states a position», «this section enumerates rules», «this section lists rejected alternatives», and so on. The chips solve a real reader problem (the pages mix modes heavily), but their meaning and when to use which chip lives nowhere except in the precedent of other pages.

A reader recently said «не понимаю, в каком жанре эта страница» about traceability-matrix.html; the page was hybrid — partly Explanation (theory, worked example), partly Reference (the live matrix with status pills). The fix split the two genres into two pages, and that fix is the right pattern going forward, but we need to ratify it so future SWE / QA handbooks do not regress to «everything under reference/» on day one.

Decision

The conventions below are normative for every handbook under foundations/reference/<discipline>/:

D1 · Quadrant: every handbook page is Explanation

Pages under foundations/reference/<discipline>/ describe methodology, principles, contracts, rationale, and worked examples — Diátaxis Explanation in spirit. They must declare data-page-type="explanation" on <body>. The folder name is a deliberate accident of IA history (handbooks are «reference material» in the colloquial sense) and we accept the mismatch in exchange for not breaking ~50 cross-links and the existing hub cards.

D2 · Lens chip vocabulary — 7 lenses, 1 chip per section

Every <section class="numbered-section"> on a handbook page carries exactly one lens chip:

<p class="lens-chip-row">
  <span class="lens-chip" data-lens="rules">
    <span class="lens-chip__dot"></span>Rules
  </span>
        </p>

Valid data-lens values and when to use each:

LensWhen the section is…Typical content
stance…a position the handbook commits to«We do X, not Y.» Aphorism + 1–2 paragraphs of stance.
rules…a normative enumerationNumbered or bulleted rules the reviewer enforces. The most common lens.
rationale…the reasoning behind a rule or trade-offWhy we picked X over Y. Trade-off cards / wins-vs-costs.
anti…rejected patterns«Anti-patterns reviewers reject» — what NOT to do, with one-line reason each.
apply…actionable guidance / pointers«What to read next», «Where the live X lives», follow-up cards.
exemplars…a worked exampleOne concrete case end-to-end. Teaching IDs (e.g. REQ-EX-01) preferred over real data.
explain…background / mental modelWhat a term means, how a system fits together. Sparingly — most explanation lives in the page lede.

D3 · Every page closes with a «What to read next»

The Explanation quadrant in Diátaxis requires a closer (E#6 of the audit rubric). Every handbook page ends with a final numbered section labelled «What to read next», chip data-lens="apply", containing 2–4 follow-up cards covering at least one of the other three Diátaxis quadrants (Reference, How-to, Tutorial) plus one related Explanation page.

D4 · Reference data lives in governance/

When a handbook page describes a system that has live, mutable data (a traceability matrix with current status pills, an ownership directory with current paging rotations, an audit log with current findings), the live data is rendered as a separate Reference page under governance/<area>/. The handbook page is the contract; the governance page is the snapshot. The handbook stays stable while the snapshot churns.

D5 · Lens vocabulary is closed

The seven lenses above are the canonical set. Adding an eighth lens requires a follow-up ADR superseding D5 — not a unilateral commit. Reviewers reject a PR introducing a new data-lens value.

Scope

  • In scope:
    • handbook/ — SA handbook, ~24 pages, first audit target.
    • foundations/reference/swe/ — SWE handbook (currently empty, will grow on this canon).
    • foundations/handbook/qa/ — QA handbook (same).
    • foundations/reference/dev/ — Dev handbook (same).
  • Out of scope:
    • governance/ — Reference quadrant for live data (matrices, audits, decisions). Lens chips do not apply.
    • foundations/explanation/ — cross-discipline Explanation (e.g. reader-personas.html); follows Diátaxis canon naturally without lens-chip overlay.
    • Public portal under services/portal/public/ — its own Diátaxis surface (Tutorials · How-to · Reference · Explanation), see the 2026-06-06 audit.
    • Per-service pages under services/<svc>/ — ADR 0026 governs those.

Alternatives considered MANDATORY · MIN 2

  1. Move all Explanation-style handbook pages to foundations/explanation/<discipline>/.
    • Rejected because: breaks ~50 internal cross-links across hubs, breadcrumbs, the just-merged sidebar grouping (System modeling / Governance / Format contracts), and the SA handbook's hub-card narrative. The disk-vs-quadrant purity gain is real but bounded; the breakage cost is large and recurring (every external page linking to handbook/X.html would need a redirect or a rewrite).
    • Pros considered: clean Diátaxis on file-tree inspection; new reviewers don't need to know the «folder name lies» convention.
    • Cons that decided it: link breakage at scale, plus the same problem returns the moment a new discipline lands and someone forgets to repeat the move. The implicit fix (folder name lies, body attribute tells the truth) is robust under inattention.
  2. Drop lens chips, rely on plain section structure.
    • Rejected because: the chips solve a measured reader problem. Handbook sections mix rhetorical modes heavily — one page can carry a stance, a rule, a trade-off, an anti-pattern, and a worked example back to back. Without the chip, a reviewer scanning «is this the rule or the rationale?» loses ~30 seconds per section; over a 24-page audit pass that compounds to half an hour of avoidable friction.
    • Pros considered: smaller vocabulary, no «what lens does this section get?» author overhead.
    • Cons that decided it: in practice the author overhead is small (the chip is one line; mapping section-purpose → chip is mechanical) while the reader productivity gain is repeated on every read.
  3. Document the conventions only in style-guide.html without an ADR.
    • Rejected because: a style-guide section is non-binding — it documents «how things are written», not «why we made this choice». Six months later, when the SWE handbook is being authored and reviewers ask «can we use the simpler 4-Diátaxis-quadrant approach?», there is no immutable record of why the current pattern was chosen. The handbook then regresses to whatever the new author prefers.
    • Pros considered: lighter-weight, no ADR overhead, easier to evolve.
    • Cons that decided it: ADRs exist precisely because style-guide conventions drift; the cost of a 1-hour ADR write-up is small compared to a quarter of handbook regression.

Consequences

Positive

  • Future SWE / QA / Dev handbooks follow the same shape on day one without re-debating Diátaxis quadrant.
  • Reviewers can demand chips and the «What to read next» closer in PR review with a one-line citation («ADR 0031, D2 / D3»).
  • The hybrid contract-vs-coverage split (D4) prevents drift: handbook pages stay stable while live data lives where it can churn.
  • External Diátaxis auditors see a written rationale for «Explanation under reference/», not implicit convention.
  • Closing the lens vocabulary (D5) prevents lens proliferation — the chip set will not grow to 12 by next quarter.

Trade-offs

  • «Explanation under reference/» looks wrong on file-tree inspection. Acknowledged anti-pattern, accepted cost; mitigated by the body attribute being the source of truth and by this ADR existing.
  • 7 lens labels add cognitive overhead. More than the 4 Diátaxis quadrants. Mitigated by the lens-chip component rendering a tooltip with the lens definition on hover.
  • Every new handbook page now carries two extra obligations — page-type attribute and per-section chips. Mechanical, but enforced in review, not by CI (yet — see Validation).

Compatibility and migration

  • Backward-compatibility impact: low. Existing SA handbook pages already implicitly follow the pattern (the ADR ratifies what's there). The audit will find ~5–10 pages missing one of the three obligations (page-type · chips · closer); each fix is additive, no content rewrite.
  • Migration plan:
    1. Single-pass audit of handbook/ against D1–D4 (per-page fix-list, similar to the public portal audit on 2026-06-06).
    2. Apply fixes page-by-page on a feature branch.
    3. Add an SA-handbook page (handbook/handbook-conventions.html or a section in style-guide.html) that operationalises this ADR for daily reference.
    4. When SWE / QA / Dev handbooks are authored, this ADR is the contract reviewers cite.
  • Rollback strategy: mechanical — revert page-type attributes + delete <p class="lens-chip-row"> nodes. Chips are additive markup, not structural rewrites; a 24-page revert is <1 hour.

Implementation plan

  1. Ratify this ADR (Proposed → Accepted).
  2. Run a single-pass audit of handbook/ against D1–D4. Output: per-page fix-list (status · page-type missing · chips missing · closer missing).
  3. Apply fixes page-by-page; each PR commit references «ADR 0031».
  4. Add a one-page lens-vocabulary cheat-sheet under handbook/ (or extend style-guide.html) — readable canonical reference of D2 / D3 for daily authoring.
  5. When the live traceability matrix lands under governance/traceability/, it is the first concrete D4 split.

Validation

  • Manual review (today): reviewers cite ADR 0031 D1 / D2 / D3 in PR comments on any handbook page change. Acceptable until the CI checks below land.
  • CI check (deferred, post-ratification): tools/docs/validate_docs_html.py grows two assertions for paths matching services/portal/internal/foundations/reference/<discipline>/*.html:
    1. Body declares data-page-type="explanation".
    2. Every <section class="numbered-section"> contains exactly one <span class="lens-chip" data-lens="…"> whose value is in the closed set of seven.
    3. Last numbered section has id="next-h" (or equivalent data-lens="apply" closer).
  • Documentation updates: style-guide.html gains a one-paragraph pointer to this ADR; the SA-handbook hub (handbook/index.html) carries an inline link to the lens-vocabulary cheat-sheet (item 4 of Implementation plan).
