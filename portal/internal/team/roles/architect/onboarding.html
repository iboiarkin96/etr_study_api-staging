Architect onboarding — 60-day journey

Your first 60 days as an architect on Study App, mapped to concrete reads and concrete deliverables. Each step has a time horizon, a short description, the canonical pages to read, and one outcome — the thing you will be able to do when you have finished the step.

Work the steps in order. The first three are compulsory; the rest are strongly encouraged. By the end you will have driven an architecture review independently — that is the bar.

The journey · six milestones

  1. 01
    Day 1 · access

    Get into the project

    Clone the monorepo, install the toolchain, get the right accounts (GitLab, observability, ADR repo), and confirm you can reach the staging environment and render the C4 diagrams. Nothing exciting — but the next five steps depend on this one working.

    • Local development
    • System design overview
    • C4 model

    Outcome. You can open staging, render the project's C4 diagrams, and locate the ADR archive.

  2. 02
    Days 2–3 · foundations

    Read the why before the what

    Understand how we make architectural decisions, which principles are non-negotiable, and how boundaries are drawn. Engineering principles are the constraints; DDD is the modelling lens; hexagonal is the default app architecture you will be asked to defend.

    • Engineering principles
    • Domain-driven design
    • Hexagonal architecture
    • NFR definition

    Outcome. You can name the project's five most important NFRs and the architectural style chosen to meet them.

  3. 03
    Week 1 · the way around

    Learn how decisions flow

    Sit in on one architecture review and one RFC discussion. Read the last ten ADRs from start to finish — they are the shortest path to project context. Open three open RFCs and trace each from problem framing to the merge decision.

    • ADR process
    • RFC process
    • Architecture review

    Outcome. Given any open RFC, you can name its current stage, its decision owner, and what it needs to merge.

  4. 04
    Week 2 · first ticket

    Write your first ADR

    Pick one small open decision from the radar (your buddy will point you at one). Write it up as an ADR: context, options, decision, consequences. Add a C4 container diagram if the decision changes boundaries. Get one peer review, merge.

    • Design docs
    • PoC + spike
    • Build vs buy

    Outcome. You have authored and merged one ADR (with C4 update where applicable) on a real decision.

  5. 05
    Weeks 3–4 · first deliverable

    Own one full design doc

    Take a real upcoming feature and author its design doc end-to-end: context, NFRs, options, chosen design, fitness functions, risks, threat model. Drive the design review with SA, dev, QA, and SRE.

    • Fitness functions
    • Threat modeling
    • API-first design
    • Event-driven architecture

    Outcome. You have shipped one signed-off design doc with NFRs, fitness functions, and a threat model on a real feature.

  6. 06
    Day 60 · sign-off

    Own your first architecture review

    You are now the named architect on a major change. Drive the review: walk the C4 deltas, defend the NFRs, surface the risks, decide adopt/trial/assess for any new tech. The buddy stays available but does not facilitate. When the review is signed off — that is the end of onboarding.

    • Technology radar
    • Technical roadmapping
    • Architecture retrospective

    Outcome. You have driven and signed off one architecture review independently. You are now a fully ramped architect on Study App.
