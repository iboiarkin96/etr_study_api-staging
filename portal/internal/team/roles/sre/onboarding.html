SRE onboarding — 60-day journey

Your first 60 days as an SRE on Study App, mapped to concrete reads and concrete deliverables. Each step has a time horizon, a short description, the canonical pages to read, and one outcome — the thing you will be able to do when you have finished the step.

Work the steps in order. The first three are compulsory; the rest are strongly encouraged. By the end you will have taken one on-call shift solo — that is the bar.

The journey · six milestones

  1. 01
    Day 1 · access

    Get into the project

    Clone the monorepo, install the toolchain, get the right accounts (GitLab, observability stack, PagerDuty, dashboards), and confirm you can reach the staging environment and open every primary dashboard. Nothing exciting — but the next five steps depend on this one working.

    • Local development
    • Observability overview
    • Monitoring

    Outcome. You can open every primary dashboard, locate the alert catalogue, and find the on-call rota.

  2. 02
    Days 2–3 · foundations

    Read the why before the what

    Understand which SLOs we run, what an error budget is, and how on-call is structured. SLOs are the contract with the rest of the company; the on-call handbook tells you how the rota works; incident response tells you what to do when the budget burns.

    • SLOs & error budgets
    • On-call
    • Incident response
    • On-call handbook

    Outcome. You can name the project's top three SLOs, their burn-rate thresholds, and the alert that fires for each.

  3. 03
    Week 1 · the way around

    Learn how incidents flow

    Shadow a senior SRE through one full incident and one postmortem write-up. Read the runbook index — that is your first port of call when an alert fires. Open three recent postmortems and trace each from detection to follow-up tickets.

    • Incident command
    • Blameless postmortem
    • Runbook index

    Outcome. Given any open incident, you can name its command roles, its severity, and the next step the IC is expecting.

  4. 04
    Week 2 · first ticket

    Walk through one runbook

    Pick a low-risk runbook (your buddy will point you at one), run it line by line against staging, and verify each check still works. If the runbook is stale, file a fix PR. Practice the debugging loop on a synthetic failure your buddy plants for you.

    • Debugging
    • Logging
    • Runbooks practice

    Outcome. You have executed one runbook end-to-end on staging and either confirmed it works or shipped a fix PR.

  5. 05
    Weeks 3–4 · first deliverable

    Shadow one on-call rotation

    Shadow a full on-call week. Respond alongside the primary, take notes on every page, and write a brief retro at the end. Run one game-day exercise with the team so you can practice incident command without real stakes.

    • Game days
    • Alerting design
    • Rollback playbook
    • Incident severity

    Outcome. You have shadowed one full on-call week and led one game-day exercise as IC.

  6. 06
    Day 60 · sign-off

    Take your first solo on-call shift

    You are now the primary on a real on-call rotation. Respond to alerts, run runbooks, declare incidents, write up postmortems for anything sev-3 or worse. The buddy stays available as secondary but does not click first. When the shift closes — that is the end of onboarding.

    • SLO & error budget
    • Deployment health
    • Capacity planning

    Outcome. You have closed one solo on-call shift independently. You are now a fully ramped SRE on Study App.
