Person card

Linkable tile for one human contributor. Used to build people directories (Hall of Contributors), team rosters, ownership grids, and "who maintains this?" strips. Distinct from Card (generic surface) and the compact inline data-component="author-chip" (history rows, footnotes).

When to use

  • Person card — large tile inside a roster grid. Avatar is the focal point. Use for the Hall of Contributors, team page, and ownership directories.
  • Author chip (existing) — inline pill inside prose / history rows. Use for "edited by", "owned by", footer history.
  • Card — anything not centered on a person. Don't put avatars on a plain docs-card; that's what this component exists for.

Variant · spotlight

Use .docs-person-card--spotlight when one person needs to stand out from the rest of a roster — a section lead, a featured contributor, a "pinned" card. Accent halo always on, faint accent-tinted surface — communicates "primary" without shouting.

Variant · horizontal

For compact roster rows inside a sidebar, aside, or "people who own this page" callout. Avatar shrinks to 72 px and moves left of the body. Collapses to the default vertical layout on ≤760 px.

The grid container is .docs-person-grid (240 px floor) or .docs-person-grid--narrow (200 px floor for denser rosters). Wraps automatically — no need for a media query.

Filtering by domain

Use the canonical .cockpit-toolbar > .cockpit-filter-row pattern documented on the Chip page. Add data-tags="…" to each person card — filter-chips.js will toggle [data-filter-hidden] automatically.

States

  • Default — bg-elev surface, 1 px --line border, no shadow.
  • Hover / focus — accent halo fades in around the avatar, border tints to accent, card lifts 2 px.
  • Spotlight — modifier --spotlight: faint accent surface + always-on halo.
  • Horizontal — modifier --horizontal: avatar left, 72 px; reverts to vertical at ≤760 px.

A11y

  • Whole tile is one <a> — single tap target, no nested links inside.
  • Avatar <img alt=""> is decorative (the name follows immediately).
  • Focus ring is the standard 2 px accent outline at 2 px offset — never removed.
  • Domain chips are presentational — wrap them in <ul> with aria-label.
  • Reduced motion: halo, hover lift, and avatar ring transitions are all suppressed.

Tokens used

  • --card / --bg-elev, --bg-mute, --line, --line-soft
  • --accent, --accent-2, --accent-3 (halo conic-gradient)
  • --radius-lg, --shadow-md
  • --space-1..--space-5
  • --fs-200..--fs-500, --font-mono (role + handle)
  • --ease-out · 200 / 240 ms (entry, hover)
