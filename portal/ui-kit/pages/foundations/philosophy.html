Foundations

Design philosophy

A premium-restrained, material-grounded canon. Decorative noise is the enemy; information is the product. Every surface earns its weight.

Five principles

1 · Restraint over decoration

canon
Colour and motion carry meaning, not vibe. Accent is a signal — overuse erases the signal. If the page reads without the colour, the colour is right.

2 · One reason to look

canon
Each screen has one focal point — the primary action, the active item, the next step. Two equally loud surfaces means two diluted surfaces.

3 · Density with breath

canon
Information density is a feature; cramped layouts are not. Use the space scale (--space-1..--space-8) — never arbitrary px. White space carries hierarchy.

4 · System over snowflake

canon
Every pattern that appears twice becomes a component. Inline styles outside <demo>-wrappers are technical debt. If you cannot name the block, you should not ship it.

5 · Motion that informs

canon
Every animation answers a question: where did this come from? where did it go? what changed? Animation without an answer is friction. prefers-reduced-motion always wins.

What we don't do

Don'tWhy
Drop-shadow billboards on every cardElevation should mean something. Flat surfaces with hairline borders read cleaner.
Decorative gradients without roleGradients are a brand signal — reserve for hero, accent, mesh-bg. Never on body text or controls.
Two accents on one surfaceIf two things are accent, neither is. Pick one.
Multiple <h1>One page = one subject. The h1 is the page's promise to the reader.
Inline styles outside demo wrappersOne-off styling cannot be themed, refactored, or audited. Move it to a token + component.
Emoji as functional UIDecorative only. Functional icons must be SVG so they theme, scale, and a11y-label correctly.
Mermaid diagramsPer ADR 0020 — diagrams are PlantUML. Mermaid is forbidden across the portal.

Material grounding, premium restraint

The kit's grounding is material — surfaces, elevation, motion that respects physics. Its restraint is premium — high contrast, generous space, minimal chrome. The two together make information feel handled rather than decorated. We are not a marketing surface; we are a working surface for engineering and product.

If a screen feels noisy, the answer is almost always subtract, not add.

How this shows up in the kit

  • Tokens — colour, space, type, motion, elevation as a single source of truth.
  • CSS architecture — BEM + token cascade. No magic numbers.
  • JS modules — small, mount-once, idempotent, no framework.
  • Motion — easings, durations, reduced-motion contract.
  • Hotkeys — keyboard-first; mouse is a fallback.
  • Components — every visible block, with a11y rules and tokens it consumes.

Precedents we read, not copy

The kit's posture borrows from a small set of references — Stripe's documentation density, Linear's information hierarchy, Vercel's hero restraint, Anthropic's prose typography. None of these are reproduced visually; what we take is the grammar: how chrome stays out of the way, how accent is rationed, how motion is earned.

Live by it

Every PR that touches a visible surface answers two questions in the description:

  1. Which principle from this page is it serving?
  2. Which forbidden pattern did you resist?

If neither has a clean answer, the PR is reviewing itself.
